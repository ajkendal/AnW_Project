Amanda Kendal-Brown

Amanda is a Software Engineer for Textura, located in Downtown Chicago. She got her undergraduate degree in Music from the University of Iowa. As a music major Amanda had the opportunity to also play with The Colts Drum and Bugle Corps for two seasons where she marched Baritone both years, and has also performed with Tampa Bay Thunder Drum and Bugle Corps in the front ensemble. After she finished her undergrad, Amanda had the opportunity to participate in the world’s largest steel band competition, located in Trinidad and Tobago, playing the bass for Caribbean Airlines Invaders Steel Orchestra.

After college she worked at the VA Medical Center as a data manager for one year, then decided to move back to the Midwest to go to graduate school. Previously working for FedEx and attending Northeastern Illinois University to complete her Masters in Computer Science, Amanda has also had the time to skate for Windy City Roller Derby, and participated in the Roller Derby World Cup 2013 held in Dallas, Texas for Team West Indies.
